$ cat problem.md

Finance staff processed hundreds of expense receipts monthly for SAP Concur. Each receipt required manual reading, category assignment, and field entry (date, amount, merchant, tax, currency). High volume, repetitive, error-prone.

Goal: Automate receipt → SAP Concur data entry end-to-end.

$ cat pipeline.md

The pipeline has 4 stages in sequence:

  • Stage 1 — CNN Classifier: lightweight CNN classifies receipt image as travel / hotel / unknown. ~1,000 labelled images, augmented for real-world photo conditions.
  • Stage 2 — OCR (EasyOCR): text detection + recognition, returns flat list of (text, bounding_box) pairs.
  • Stage 3 — Type-specific Extraction: travel → regex + spatial rules; hotel → bounding-box table reconstruction to aggregate itemized rows.
  • Stage 4 — Validator: business rules (amount threshold, date validity, currency support, mandatory fields, OCR confidence). Failures → manual review queue.

$ cat why_classify_first.md

Hotel folios and travel receipts have fundamentally different layouts. A single model trained on mixed data achieved ~45% on hotel folios — it confused itemized room rate lines with the total. Separating by type first and using type-specific extraction logic pushed hotel accuracy to ~85%.

⚡ Build-vs-Buy Post-Mortem

Finance chose a commercial SaaS for receipt processing. Our POC had tangible value: we defined the accuracy benchmarks used to evaluate SaaS vendors, and the eventual vendor selection was informed by our field extraction tests. The technical work shaped the buy decision.
